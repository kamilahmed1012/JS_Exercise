Why she matters today

Ada's ideas about algorithms, machines, and abstract manipulation of symbols are foundational to modern computing. Beyond the specific algorithm she wrote, her vision of computers as more than calculators — machines that could manipulate symbols and compose complex processes — presaged software, data, and the concept of programmability.
